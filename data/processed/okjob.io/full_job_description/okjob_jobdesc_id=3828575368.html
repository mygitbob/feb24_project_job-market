Description

About Us and Why We're Hiring

We're YNAB ("why-nab"), a financial education company with a money management app. We teach four habits that change your relationship with money—one where you love how you spend and celebrate how you save. For nearly two decades, people have been using YNAB and then telling their friends what a difference it has made in their lives. Check out our community on Facebook, TikTok, or Reddit (really!), or read some of our app reviews, and you'll see what we mean. We love building something that has a huge positive impact on people's lives.

It will come as no surprise that we're all passionate about finances over here, but our Accounting Specialist, Lauren, takes the cake. Lauren's passion and dedication to excellence, along with YNAB's increasing size and complexity, has turned her job into two and now we need—you! As our new Payroll & Bookkeeping Specialist, you'll be responsible for all spend-related areas. You'll run both stateside and international payroll, manage accounts payable, spend, and team stipends, and, as needed, assist with the month-end financials and our budget processes.

You're the person we're looking for if you continually seek ownership and growth. You relish the fact that payroll happens often, because it means you can continually iterate and make our processes more efficient and easier for your teammates. You find joy in helping others, and go out of your way to answer personal paycheck questions or explain difficult payroll situations in a way that's clear, concise, and kind. Which leads us to your next amazing quality: You're an excellent communicator. You're not the gatekeeper for what spending is and is not okay, but you excel (get it?) at helping people understand the intent of the budget. You provide spending guidance and information that empowers them to make spending decisions and complete individual and team purchases. Most of all, you're extremely detail-oriented and deadline-driven—because making sure everyone gets paid accurately and on time is no small task. We know you're up to the challenge, though!

Requirements (these are real, actual requirements)

In this role, you'll have four main responsibilities:

  • Your primary focus will be payroll. Our teammates live all over the world, so you'll need to be comfortable creating and preparing payroll for employees in the US and the UK, and independent contractors in numerous other countries. You'll ensure that they're paid on-time, accurately, and with the necessary payroll taxes and deductions withheld on their behalf.
  • You'll own the processes for all of our accounts payable, so we expect that you'll already have some experience in this area. You'll make sure all invoices are entered into our accounts payable/spend platform (Airbase), move them through the correct approval flow, and manage all vendor information.
  • You'll also be responsible for managing our team stipends. You should be skilled at creating helpful internal documentation and keeping it up to date, managing and assisting teammates with purchases in Airbase, and delivering proper reporting for the financials.
  • Finally, you'll help with the month-end financials and budget processes. These tasks may vary, but to start you can expect to assist with stipend reconciliations and updating various team expense trackers.

How You'll Work at YNAB

Now that you've met some of your potential future teammates, let's talk more about YNAB as a company. Here's how we operate:

  • Live (Almost) Anywhere You Want: We've always been a fully remote team, and have people all over the world. For this particular position, however, we're only considering candidates based in the United States. Anywhere in the U.S. is fair game though. Just make sure you have a reliable internet connection.
  • Work Four Day Week: We've adopted a four-day work week (still 100% paid!) and rarely work more than that. There are occasions and seasons where things get busy and people put some extra time in—but then we encourage them to take some extra time off, too. We work hard and smart and we care deeply about what we do, but we also love our families and about 2,000 other things. We have perspective and, ultimately, we think it makes us—and our work—even better.
  • Flex Your Work Schedule: We're fully remote, so a lot of our work is done asynchronously, but we love working together in real-time when it makes sense. We try to schedule most meetings between 12-3pm Eastern time Monday-Thursday. Outside of your meetings, we trust you to set your own schedule by balancing your team's needs with your own needs. You don't need to ask for permission to take off early one afternoon to see the doctor, or be "active" on Slack if you're working deeply on a project. We look at what you accomplish—not when or how long you're in front of a computer.
  • Take Vacation (Seriously): We want you to take vacation. In fact, we have a minimum vacation policy of three weeks per year. Five weeks feels about right (plus two extra weeks for our company-wide December Break). It's important to get plenty of downtime and to get out and do something. We'll look forward to seeing pictures of your adventures in our #office-wall Slack channel!
