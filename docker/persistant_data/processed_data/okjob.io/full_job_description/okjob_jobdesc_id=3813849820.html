The Role:

Digible, Inc. is looking for a Principal Software Engineer to join our team!

The Principal Software Engineer reports to the CTO and is a peer to management leadership. This role involves partnering with engineers and leadership to influence and drive technical strategy across the organization, including Data and Engineering domains. This role is of the “Architect” archetype (following Will Larson’s “Staff Engineer Archetypes” model) helping our team move from having built successful early products to creating a durable product platform that accelerates our momentum into the future.

You will zoom out when developing technical strategies, mentoring engineering leads across the team, and leading efforts to establish standards and technology selection. You will periodically zoom in and contribute to specific deliverables within a given project or team to ensure success and stay connected to the day-to-day work.

As a team, we come from many different backgrounds, countries, and fields of education. We strongly believe this diversity and agility has helped us build an inclusive and empathetic environment, and we are looking forward to adding your perspective!

You’ll love this job if you:

  • Are a curious person with a customer-first mentality
  • Know the value of a well-articulated and well-communicated technical plan
  • Invest in and sponsor your colleagues to see them grow and succeed
  • Have seen many ways teams and products fail and are excited to help us avoid common mistakes
  • Enjoy bringing Data and Engineering closer together
  • Are excited to work with passionate and invested colleagues

What you’ll do:

  • Drive broad technical strategy and influence engineering roadmaps
  • Collaboratively establish a consistent approach to building applications and services
  • Deliver strategically important elements of our roadmap
  • Standardize and centralize our infrastructure footprint
  • Imbue a Product Mindset within the team
  • Increase team velocity and ability to ship high-quality features quickly
  • Facilitate outcomes-oriented thinking, planning, and communicating
  • Improve partnerships and relationships with stakeholders

You should have:

  • 8-10+ years of engineering experience with at least 3 years in roles leading other engineers (e.g., team lead, lead engineer, technical lead)
  • Experience working in both small (50-150) and medium to large (500+) companies
  • Background in product development, with experience in both platform and product development being a plus
  • Experience with modern javascript-based web stacks (Node, TypeScript, React, etc.), system design, and data-driven application best practices
