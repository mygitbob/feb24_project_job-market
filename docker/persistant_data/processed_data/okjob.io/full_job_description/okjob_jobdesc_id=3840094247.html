About the role:

As the Studio Manager you will be responsible for the successful management of resources and planning of workflow for our graphic design studio. You will provide crucial support for the business and the studio, ensuring work is distributed fairly, that projects are delivered efficiently, on time and on budget.

A studio manager plays a crucial role in ensuring the smooth and efficient operation of the studio and acts as a liaison between the creative team and clients. They manage expectations, and ensure that project deliverables align with client needs. Their responsibilities encompass a range of tasks related to project management, team coordination, and overall workflow optimisation.

What you will do:

  • Project Management/Scheduling and Planning: Studio managers are responsible for creating and maintaining project schedules. They ensure that projects are delivered on time by setting realistic timelines, assigning tasks, and tracking progress.
  • Resource Allocation: They allocate resources such as manpower, equipment, and workspace efficiently to meet project requirements and deadlines.
  • Budgeting: Studio managers often play a role in managing project budgets, tracking expenses, and ensuring that projects are completed within financial constraints.
  • Workflow Optimisation/Process Improvement: Studio managers continuously evaluate and refine workflow processes to enhance efficiency and productivity.
  • Problem Solving/Issue Resolution: When challenges or conflicts arise during a project, studio managers are responsible for resolving issues promptly and effectively.
  • Administrative Tasks/Documentation: Studio managers often handle administrative tasks such as documentation, reporting, and record-keeping.

Preferred Skills:

  • Experience of working within design or creative agencies (8 years or more)
  • Having an understanding or experience in Events
  • Excellent communicator, positive but realistic with the ability to judge the appropriate way to communicate problems according to the audience.
  • Calm under pressure and skilled in managing peak periods in the lead up to events.
  • Experience in using Asana or familiar using any other work management platform
  • Supplying reports to outline progress and flag any foreseen challenges regarding time and resources.
  • Able to plan the creative studio projects one year in advance to create transparency across the business regarding resources.

In essence, a studio manager is a key figure in the successful execution of creative projects. Their ability to balance the creative aspects of the work with organisational and managerial responsibilities is crucial for the overall success of the agency.
